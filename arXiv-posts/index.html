How can we unravel the merger history of the Milky Way? Viterbo & Buck explore the use of simulation-based inference on measured stellar chemical abundances 1/6

Fundamentally it appears to be a method of finding the best way to combine subhalos from the various NIHAO zoom-in cosmological simulations to make the chemical distribution of the galaxy's halo. 2/6

This uses a variety of machine-learning techniques and leverages the fact that there is a relationship between the mass of an incoming galaxy and its stars' chemical abundances. 3/6

I worry that the fundamental limitation here will be that the simulations have applied some prescription to change the abundances of forming stars (which will be imperfect) and subject to stochastic effects. 4/6

This will get you the best set of subhalos under those very specific circumstances, but that's not taking the physics of it into account. I always get worried when error bars all overlap the true value. 5/6

Still, maybe I'm just a bit of a fuddy-duddy about some uses of ML, but I'm glad that people are trying to widen the scope of ways we might use it. 6/6
